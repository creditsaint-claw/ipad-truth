Your Brain

Your brain is still
being built.

The front part of your brain — the part that controls judgment, focus, and making good decisions — doesn't finish growing until you're 25. At 11, it's in one of its most critical construction phases. Heavy screen time is actively disrupting that construction.

🧠

Brain Scan Evidence

The ABCD Study scanned 10,000+ kids with MRI. Heavy screen users showed visible thinning in the attention and memory centers of their brain. Not a theory — visible on an actual brain scan.

🎮

Your Reward System Gets Broken

Heavy gamers showed reduced activity in the brain's reward center. Your brain starts needing more and more stimulation to feel the same pleasure. Real life starts feeling boring by comparison.

💭

Working Memory Shrinks

Kids aged 6–11 with high screen time showed measurably worse working memory in a 2026 study of thousands of children. Working memory is how you hold information while you think — it affects every subject in school.

⚡

Attention Gets Fried

Fast-paced content trains your brain to expect constant stimulation every few seconds. After enough of that, sitting through a class, reading a book, or having a conversation feels unbearably slow. That's not a personality trait — that's damage.

Your Eyes

Ages 8–13 is when nearsightedness
locks in permanently.

You are in the exact window where myopia (nearsightedness) develops fastest. Screen time is one of the documented causes — combined with not getting enough natural outdoor light. Once you're nearsighted, you wear glasses for the rest of your life.

👁️

2.3× Higher Risk of Nearsightedness

A 2024 meta-analysis covering 19 studies found that heavy screen-using children have 2.24–2.39× higher odds of developing myopia than low-users. The good news: 1–2 hours of outdoor time daily has strong evidence for slowing it. Natural light regulates how your eye grows. That's a real, proven fix.

Your Body

Tech neck is real.
You're probably doing it right now.

When you look down at an iPad, you're putting up to 60 lbs of pressure on your neck and spine — that's like carrying a 10-year-old on your head. Do that for 4+ hours a day for years and you're setting yourself up for chronic pain.

🦴

Neck & Back Pain

Research documented increases in cervical (neck) and lumbar (lower back) pain in kids and adolescents directly linked to portable device use. Habits you set at 11 follow you into adulthood.

🏃

Physical Inactivity

Every hour on the iPad is an hour not running, jumping, or building muscle. Your heart, lungs, and muscles need movement during these years to develop properly. Screen time directly displaces that.

🖐️

Fine Motor Trade-Off

Excessive touchscreen use reduces time spent on pencil-grip, building, drawing, and sports skills — all of which develop fine and gross motor pathways your brain needs.

What Actually Works

How to take back
control.

The research points to specific, proven interventions. These aren't punishments — they're how you protect your own brain.

1

No screens 1 hour before bed — non-negotiable

Your brain needs that hour to produce melatonin naturally. Even dim screen light suppresses it. This one change improves sleep quality more than almost anything else.

2

Device stays out of the bedroom overnight

If it's in your room, you'll reach for it at 2am. That's not willpower — that's how the product is designed. Physical distance is the only reliable solution.

3

Get 1–2 hours outside every day

Proven to slow myopia progression. Proven to release dopamine naturally. Proven to improve sleep. One of the most researched and underrated interventions available.

4

Replace passive watching with active creating

Coding, building, drawing, music — active screen use has measurably different outcomes. If you're going to be on a screen, make something instead of just consuming.

5

Understand what autoplay and infinite scroll are doing

They are specifically designed to remove the moment where you'd naturally stop. Knowing this lets you choose to stop deliberately — before the algorithm decides for you.
